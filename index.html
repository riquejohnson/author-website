The Author

behind the Pen

"Every story is a confession of what we understand about humanity."

Rique Johnson is the author of the acclaimed detective series, featuring Virginia City's complex and passionate Detective Jason Jerrard — a man whose personal life is as turbulent as the crimes he pursues. The series showcases Johnson's ability to weave heart-pounding thriller elements with deeply felt emotional landscapes.

Born and raised in Portsmouth, Virginia, Johnson served in the U.S. Army before channeling his experiences, observations, and imagination into fiction. His writing spans the full range of human experience: from the electric tension of a detective closing in on a killer, to the quiet vulnerability of a man incapable of commitment, to the measured rhythms of poetry that lay the soul bare.

His work appears both as solo novels — published through Strebor Books and Simon & Schuster — and in acclaimed anthologies curated by bestselling authors including Zane, who selected Johnson as a featured contributor for Another Time, Another Place.

His poetry exists alongside his prose fiction, reflecting a creative vision that refuses to be confined to any single form or genre.

Poetry

Scent

"People often say that we should take time to smell the roses. Take a moment to enjoy the simplest things in life. I have inhaled deeply. I became captivated by your scent. Not by the fragrance of your perfume. Not by the smell of your shampoo. Simply, captivated by the aroma within you. I smell a rose every time the thought of you dances in my mind. I smell a rose every time I’m treated to the brilliance of your smile. I smell a rose every time the pleasing vibrations of your voice touch my ears. I smell a rose every time our glances become a hidden embrace. Your presence is like walking through a field of flowers. Vibrant and stimulating. Bathing me with your aura. Touching me within. I have inhaled deeply. Your scent could last a lifetime."

— Rique Johnson

Life being Itself

"She came into my life like a snowflake of a thousand shapes. Falling, windblown, descending with no purpose, no direction, yet willing to share her internal beauty. She landed, unstable into my life, willing to be served. Needing to serve. But, determined not to be bound by her own sensual desires. Hidden desires, released from their suppressed state, now flow freely through her being enabling her to become… Something new, Something bold, Something bad. Released from the notion of what sex, sexuality and serving oneself are. Armed with the knowledge that these can’t reign freely without Love. Her love is vast, expanding the very limits that define the word. Her love is intoxicating…enveloping me… Enslaving me. Controlling parts of me that I desire not to be controlled. Weakening my desire to free myself. Tapping on the door of my heart, willing me to become… Eager to explore the infinite possibilities of what the two of us could bring. I find myself, falling, windblown… Understanding that life is just being itself."

— Rique Johnson
